    I pressed "Play" on a session, but nothing happened.

    Our sessions play via live streaming, and require an active internet connection in order to play. The first step is to ensure that you are connected to a reliable Wifi or hardwire connection.

    If you are connected but are experiencing a delay between pressing "play" and the session beginning, please be aware that due to local bandwidth limitations you might need to wait between 3-30 seconds for the session to load and play on your device. The delay may be longer if you are using a mobile device.

    To avoid a delay, try these steps:

    1) Turn your Wifi connection off and back on again, then try again.

    2) Reload the page in your browser.

    3) Try connecting to a higher-bandwidth Wifi or hardwire network if possible.

    If you are still experiencing a long delay before the session starts, you might consider speaking with your local IT Administrator about troubleshooting your network's bandwidth. 

    Another work around would be to click "play" on the session a few minutes before you intend to use it with children, then pressing "pause" once the session starts playing. This will allow you to load the session audio in advance and avoid a delay when working with kids.

    What is mindfulness?

    Mindfulness simply means paying attention to the present moment with kindness and curiosity and without judgment. It’s intentionally drawing your awareness to thoughts, feelings, or sensations happening from moment to moment.

    What is the scientific evidence behind mindfulness in education?

    Research examining mindfulness-based approaches in education has found:

    • Improvements in student attention
    • Improvements in student social-emotional skills (perspective-taking and empathy)
    • Increases in prosocial behavior (sharing and including others)
    • Decreases in aggression and symptoms of depression

    For a full bibliography of the research behind Mind Yeti, please see this blog post.

    Is mindfulness religious?

    Although some religions, such as Hinduism, incorporate mindfulness, it’s not tied to religion. Mindfulness is a natural state of consciousness, and the intentional practice of mindfulness can train attention. Mind Yeti is a secular product to help kids practice mindfulness.

    Do I need training in mindfulness to lead mindfulness practice in my home or classroom?

    Mind Yeti was created for individuals new to mindfulness and those who have previous mindfulness experience.

    Mind Yeti was developed so you can participate in the sessions with your students/child(ren). What this means is you don’t have to be an expert! Let Mind Yeti lead the sessions so you can share the experience with the young people in your life.

    We suggest you take some time to familiarize yourself with the Mind Yeti website and introductory sessions. This will assist you in preparing to introduce Mind Yeti to your students/child(ren). Take a look at the Mind Yeti introduction and try out one or two Open Breath Awareness sessions (found in CALM) to get a sense of Mind Yeti.

    How frequently should I do Mind Yeti sessions?

    Mindfulness practice trains the brain for improved attention, emotion regulation, and stress management. Just as regular exercise strengthens the body, regular mindfulness sessions strengthen the mind!

    The more consistently and frequently you practice, the better! We recommend daily Mind Yeti practice. Several times a day is optimal.

    What age range does this serve? Are there modifications for younger or older children?

    Mind Yeti is designed to move at a pace determined by you and your students/child(ren). The beta version of Mind Yeti was developed for elementary-school-age children. However, the product can be used with a wider age range. For younger children, you may find staying with the earlier progressions in each session is most developmentally appropriate. For older children, you may find that once you‘ve completed the early progressions, spending more time with the later progressions is more appropriate.

    I work with diverse learners. Are there any special modifications or suggestions for implementing this for special education?

    Mind Yeti is designed to be a universal resource to help guide mindfulness practice. It follows a progressive sequence that can be modified to meet the needs of each student. For example, if a student has difficulty being still, that student could hold a fidgeting toy or stand instead of sit. You could also repeat sessions as needed to build skills before moving on to the next progression.

    If a child has been traumatized, is it still okay to use Mind Yeti sessions?

    Mind Yeti should be presented as an invitation, not a requirement. Children who have experienced trauma need to feel safe at school. If, for example, they don’t feel comfortable closing their eyes for a Mind Yeti session, that's okay. Let them practice with their eyes open. It's important to respect each child's needs. If a child has mental health issues, consult with his or her counselor about the best ways to introduce new activities.
